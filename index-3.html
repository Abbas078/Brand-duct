Principles of our work

What materials are best for cooling ducts?

Galvanized steel is durable and long-lasting, while flexible ducting is ideal for tight spaces. Fiberglass ducts offer excellent insulation, and all materials should be chosen based on the building’s requirements.

Why is duct insulation important?

Insulation prevents energy loss, keeps the cooled air at the desired temperature, and reduces the risk of condensation that can lead to mold or water damage.

Can I install cooling ducts in an older building?

Yes, cooling ducts can be retrofitted into older buildings. However, it may require custom designs to accommodate existing structures.

How can I reduce energy loss in my cooling ducts?

Ensure ducts are properly sealed and insulated, avoid sharp bends in the layout, and schedule regular maintenance to check for leaks or damage.

How long do cooling ducts last?

With proper maintenance, cooling ducts can last 20–25 years. Regular inspections help identify issues that might reduce their lifespan.

How does duct design affect energy efficiency?

A poorly designed duct system can result in energy loss, uneven cooling, and higher utility bills. Proper sizing, insulation, and layout are key to maintaining efficiency.
